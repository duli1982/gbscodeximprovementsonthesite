Best Practices for Prompting

Follow these golden rules to get the best possible results from Gemini. A well-crafted prompt is the key to unlocking high-quality, relevant, and actionable outputs.

1. Give Clear Context

Tell Gemini what you're doing and set the scene clearly. The more background it has, the more relevant the response will be.

"I have a biology test in 2 days."

2. Be Specific About Output

Say exactly what you want back. Include the desired type, format, length, or structure.

"Give 10 MCQs on the circulatory system."

3. Assign the AI a Role

Define who Gemini should act as. This adds a specific tone, style, and level of expertise to the response.

"Act as a finance professor."

4. Break It Into Steps

For complex requests, break your needs into logical steps. This helps the AI follow your train of thought.

"Explain in 3 steps using an analogy."

5. Provide Examples

If you want a specific style or structure, give Gemini an example of what you're looking for.

"Give 3 ways photosynthesis helps humans."

6. Refine and Retry

Your first try is a draft. If the output isn't quite right, tweak your prompt with more clarity and try again.

Better input → better output.

7. Avoid Vague Language

Don't be general. Vague inputs lead to weak replies. Replace "help me study" with a specific request.

Skip "Help me study."

8. Treat It Like an Assistant

Write like you're briefing a new team member. Be simple, clear, and direct in your instructions.

Like giving a task to an intern.

9. Choose a Format

Explicitly tell the AI how to present the information, whether as bullets, a numbered list, a table, or prose.

"Summarize as a table."

10. Provide Negative Constraints

Sometimes it's helpful to tell the AI what *not* to do. This helps narrow its focus and avoid unwanted information.

"Write an email to the team, but do not use overly corporate jargon."

Pro-Level Prompting Examples

Garbage in, garbage out. If your prompts suck, your results will too. Here's how pros prompt Gemini.

11. Be Specific and Detailed

Transform vague requests into detailed, actionable prompts. Provide context, specify your role, set clear expectations, and include concrete deliverables.

❌ Don't say:

"Help me source candidates"

✅ Say:

"You're a senior RPO sourcing specialist at Randstad. I need 15 qualified candidates for (ROLE) at (CLIENT COMPANY) within 72 hours. Create a multi-channel sourcing strategy including: Boolean search strings for LinkedIn/Indeed, 5 alternative sourcing channels, and a candidate outreach sequence that gets 40%+ response rates. Show me the exact messaging for each touchpoint."

12. Provide Context and Expertise

Give the AI a specific role and detailed context. Include your goals, constraints, and expected deliverables to get professional-level output.

❌ Don't say:

"Schedule interviews better"

✅ Say:

"I'm coordinating interviews for 12 candidates across 3 time zones with 5 hiring managers who have conflicting calendars. Create an interview scheduling framework that: minimizes back-and-forth emails, provides candidate experience scoring, includes backup plans for no-shows, and sends automated reminders. Give me template emails for each scenario."

13. Handle Administrative Tasks

Transform general admin requests into specific workflow management tasks with clear deliverables and stakeholder considerations.

❌ Don't say:

"Handle this admin work"

✅ Say:

"You're managing RPO operations for 200+ open positions across 15 hiring managers. Design a workflow that tracks: candidate pipeline stages, client feedback loops, supplier performance metrics, and SLA compliance. Create status update templates that keep stakeholders informed without overwhelming them with details."

14. Manage Client Relations

Convert complaint handling into strategic relationship management with data-backed solutions and clear action plans.

❌ Don't say:

"Deal with this complaint"

✅ Say:

"A hiring manager is escalating because their Software Engineer position has been open 45 days with only 3 interviews scheduled. They're threatening to pull the RPO contract. Draft a response that: acknowledges their frustration, provides data-backed explanations, presents 3 immediate action items, and repositions this as a partnership opportunity. Include follow-up cadence."

15. Improve Candidate Experience

Turn vague improvement requests into systematic process optimization with measurable outcomes and client-ready documentation.

❌ Don't say:

"Improve candidate experience"

✅ Say:

"Map the entire candidate journey for our RPO process from application to offer. Identify the top 5 friction points that cause 60% candidate drop-off. For each point, provide: specific improvement tactics, implementation timeline, success metrics, and client communication strategy. Make this bulletproof for client reviews."

16. Manage Supplier Performance

Convert basic supplier management into comprehensive performance tracking systems with accountability measures and improvement frameworks.

❌ Don't say:

"Manage supplier relationships"

✅ Say:

"Create a supplier performance scorecard for our MSP program managing 25 staffing vendors. Include metrics that matter to clients: quality of candidates, time-to-submit, rate competitiveness, and diversity sourcing. Design quarterly business reviews that drive accountability and identify top/bottom performers with clear improvement plans."

Universal Pro Tips

Master these patterns for any situation - from cover letters to complex analysis.

17. Make Content Better

❌ Don't say:

"Make this better"

✅ Say:

"Analyze this (CONTENT) for: clarity (1-10), emotional impact (1-10), and actionability (1-10). Rewrite to score 10/10 on all metrics. Show me what you changed and why."

18. Write Cover Letters

❌ Don't say:

"Write me a cover letter"

✅ Say:

"You're a hiring manager at (COMPANY) who's rejected 200 applicants. I'm applying for (ROLE). Write a cover letter that makes you stop and say 'finally, someone who gets it.'"

19. Explain Complex Topics

❌ Don't say:

"Explain this simply"

✅ Say:

"Explain (COMPLEX TOPIC) using only the 1,000 most common English words. Then explain it through a metaphor involving (FAMILIAR ACTIVITY). Make a 10-year-old excited to learn more."

20. Optimize Resumes

❌ Don't say:

"Help me with my resume"

✅ Say:

"Here's the job posting: (PASTE). Here's my experience: (PASTE). Rewrite my bullets to use their exact keywords while quantifying impact with numbers that make me undeniable."

21. Professional Tone

❌ Don't say:

"Make this sound professional"

✅ Say:

"Rewrite this using the vocabulary and tone of (SPECIFIC INDUSTRY) leaders. Include 3 subtle power phrases that make me sound experienced without arrogance."

22. Edit and Improve

❌ Don't say:

"Check my grammar"

✅ Say:

"Edit this for: grammar, flow, and impact. Show me the top 3 changes that transform this from amateur to executive-level communication. Explain the psychology behind each edit."
